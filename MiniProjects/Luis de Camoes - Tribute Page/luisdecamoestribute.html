Luis Vaz de Camões

1524 - 1580

Portuguese Poet

Luís Vaz de Camões (c. 1524 or 1525 – 10 June 1580) is considered Portugal's and the Portuguese language's greatest poet. His mastery of verse has been compared to that of Shakespeare, Vondel, Homer, Virgil and Dante. He wrote a considerable amount of lyrical poetry and drama but is best remembered for his epic work Os Lusíadas (The Lusiads). His collection of poetry The Parnasum of Luís de Camões was lost during his life. The influence of his masterpiece Os Lusíadas is so profound that Portuguese is sometimes called the "language of Camões". The day of his death, 10 June OS, is Portugal's national day.

The following list is a time line of Luis de Camões' Life

  • 1524 - Lisbon - Luís de Camões is born.
  • 1531 - Coimbra - Travels to study at Coimbra, where his uncle was Chanceler.
  • 1541 - Coimbra - Camões finishes his studies at Coimbra.
  • 1542 - Lisbon - Camões goes to the Royal Court.
  • 1545 - Lisbon - End of his time in Royal Court.
  • 1547 - Ceuta - Departs to Ceuta
  • 1550 - Constância, Ribatejo - Camões is sent to a short exile for courting the King's sister.
  • 1552 - Rossio, Lisbon - Camões is arrested for killing a Royal Stables official.
  • 1553 - Lisbon - Released from jail by royal orders.
  • 1553 - Lisbon - Departs to India in the fleet of Fernão Álvares Cabral.
  • 1553 - Goa, India - Arrives in Goa and enlists on the service of Viceroy D. Afonso de Noronha.
  • 1553 - Porcá Island, South of Cochin, Malabar - Participates in the expedition to the Island of Porcá against the King of Chembe..
  • 1554 - Goa, India - Released from jail by royal orders.
  • 1553 - Lisbon - Participates on D. Afonso de Noronha's military expedition - Battles against the turks.
  • 1554 - Ormuz, Iran - Short stay in Ormuz.
  • 1554 - Goa, India - Returns from the expedition.
  • 1556 - Goa, India - Camões is arrested in Goa.
  • 1561 - Goa, India - Camões is arrested a second time in Goa.
  • 1562 - Macau, China - Appointed "High Provider of the Deceased and Absent". His function was to collect, manage and know all the causes regarding the goods of the deceased and absent which didn't name any prosecutors in their wills.
  • 1565 - Mekong River Mouth, Cambodja - While writing the epic poem "Os Lusíadas", Camões is shipwrecked on the way to Goa. Manages to save the manuscript of "Os Lusíadas" by swimming with one hand to shore and holding the manuscript out of the water.
  • 1567 - Malacca, Malaysia - Embarks on a ship to Sofala and Island of Mozambique.
  • 1569 - Island of Mozambique - Departs to Lisbon.
  • 1570 - Cascais - Arrival of Camões to Portugal.
  • 1580 - Lisbon - Luís de Camões dies at 56 in Lisbon.
